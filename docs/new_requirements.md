1. The portal hero section shall be rewritten to communicate the dual purpose of the site: the projects are personal tools the author actively uses and also resources made available to others.

2. The hero section shall avoid sounding like a generic portfolio or project listing and shall instead frame the work as evidence of a real working process.

3. The About section shall be expanded to reflect the author’s professional depth, including long-term experience in database and data engineering and a deliberate recent focus on applied AI.

4. The About section shall communicate that the projects come from deep engineering experience rather than casual experimentation.

5. The About section shall include a concise statement of engineering philosophy, emphasizing structured systems, visible pipelines, and daily use of AI as part of the workflow.

6. The portal shall include a “How I Build” section that explains the author’s development approach.

7. The “How I Build” section shall describe the use of AI-assisted development, agentic workflows, structured pipelines, and local model infrastructure.

8. The “How I Build” section shall remain concise and easily scannable.

9. Each project card shall display technology or capability tags that indicate the major technical themes demonstrated by the project.

10. Technology tags shall help visitors quickly identify skills such as pipelines, agentic systems, API integration, analytics, data visualization, client-side persistence, or related technical areas.

11. Each project card shall include a short “Why this exists” line explaining the motivation for the project.

12. The “Why this exists” line shall express the project’s practical or personal reason for being built, not just its functionality.

13. The portal shall include a visible contact path for professional outreach.

14. The contact path shall include at least one of the following: a LinkedIn link, a contact line in the About section, or an equivalent direct method of reaching the author.

15. The current “Common Themes” section shall be revised or replaced with a positioning section that more directly connects the projects to the author’s engineering point of view.

16. The revised positioning section shall explain in first-person or similarly direct language what ties the projects together.

17. The positioning section shall communicate recurring patterns such as real data in, visible pipeline, and usable output.

18. The portal shall include a “What’s Next,” roadmap note, or equivalent indicator showing that the site and projects are ongoing and actively evolving.

19. The roadmap indicator shall communicate that the portal is a living body of work rather than a finished archive.

20. The project overview experience shall remain scannable at the top level.

21. The portal should support an optional deeper project view, such as expandable cards or slide-out detail panels, for visitors who want more technical depth without leaving the page.

22. Expanded project detail views, if implemented, shall be able to show additional material such as screenshots, architecture notes, tech stack, and lessons learned.

23. The portal shall include a transparency note indicating that the site and its projects are built with AI assistance.

24. The AI transparency note shall reference tools, workflows, or local model usage in a concise and honest way.

25. Each project card shall surface evidence of maintenance or freshness.

26. Freshness indicators may include a last updated date, activity marker, or similar maintenance signal.

27. Freshness indicators shall communicate that the projects are active and not abandoned.

28. The overall portal content shall support both of its intended roles: personal resource hub and professional technology portfolio.

29. The portal content shall present the projects as practical, real-use systems rather than as isolated demos.

30. The portal shall make it easy for a technically oriented visitor to understand what was built, why it was built, how it was built, and how to contact the author.
